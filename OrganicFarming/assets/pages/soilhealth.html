SOIL HEALTH

Healthy soil is more than just dirt - it's a vibrant, living ecosystem filled with microorganisms, nutrients, and organic matter. In organic farming, soil is treated like a living partner, not just a planting surface.

When you care for your soil using compost, cover crops, crop rotation, and natural fertilizers, you build a foundation that supports strong, resilient plants and nutrient-rich harvests. Healthy soil also holds water better, resists erosion, and stores carbon -- helping fight climate change from the group up.

What is soil health?

Healthy soil is the heart of organic farming. it's not just dirt - it's a living ecosystem filled with beneficial microbes, fungi, earth worms, and nutrients that work together to grow strong, vibrant crops. When soil is rich, well-balanced, and alive, plants thrive naturally, resist disease and requires fewer external inputs.
